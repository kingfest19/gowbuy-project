{% trans "How to Create 3D Models With Your Phone" %}

{% trans "Turn your products into immersive 3D/AR experiences right from your smartphone." %}

{% trans "Quick Tip!" %}

{% blocktrans %}Creating a great 3D model requires good lighting and a steady hand. The goal is to capture your product from all angles so the AI can build a complete model.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "We recommend using apps that specialize in 'photogrammetry' or '3D scanning'." %}

{% trans "Step 1: Choose a 3D Scanning App" %}

{% trans "Several apps can turn a series of photos into a 3D model. Here are a few popular options available on iOS and Android:" %}

  • Polycam: {% trans "A powerful and user-friendly app available on both iOS and Android. It has a great 'Photo Mode' for creating high-quality models." %}
  • Scaniverse: {% trans "Excellent for iPhone and iPad users with a LiDAR sensor, but also works well on other devices." %}
  • Kiri Engine: {% trans "Another great cross-platform option that processes the photos in the cloud." %}

{% trans "Download one of these apps from your device's app store to get started." %}

{% trans "Step 2: Prepare Your Product and Space" %}

{% trans "Good preparation is key to a successful scan." %}

  • Lighting: {% trans "Use bright, even, and diffuse lighting. Avoid harsh shadows and direct sunlight. An overcast day outdoors or a well-lit room is ideal." %}
  • Surface: {% trans "Place your product on a non-reflective, textured surface. A newspaper or a textured mat works well. Avoid plain white or glossy surfaces." %}
  • Space: {% trans "Ensure you have enough space to walk all the way around your product." %}

{% trans "Step 3: Capture the Photos" %}

{% trans "Open your chosen 3D scanning app and start a new scan using the photo or photogrammetry mode." %}

  1. {% trans "Take photos in a full circle around your product at one height (e.g., eye-level)." %} {% trans "Overlap each photo by about 60-70%." %} {% trans "Most apps will show you guides." %}
  2. {% trans "Take another full circle of photos from a higher angle, looking down at the product." %}
  3. {% trans "If possible, take a third circle from a lower angle, looking up." %}
  4. {% trans "Capture the top and any intricate details separately." %}

{% blocktrans %}You should aim for at least 40-70 high-quality, sharp photos for a good result. More is often better!{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Step 4: Process and Export" %}

{% trans "Once you've taken all your photos, tell the app to process them. This may take a few minutes as it uploads the images and builds the model in the cloud." %}

{% trans "After processing is complete, inspect the 3D model in the app. If it looks good, find the 'Export' or 'Share' option." %}

  • {% trans "Choose the format:" %} GLB {% trans "or" %} GLTF. {% trans "GLB is usually the best choice as it's a single, compact file." %}
  • {% trans "Choose a texture resolution. 'Medium' or 'High' (e.g., 2K or 2048x2048) is usually a good balance of quality and file size." %}
  • {% trans "Save the exported .glb file to your device, cloud storage, or email it to yourself." %}

{% trans "Step 5: Upload to NEXUS" %}

{% trans "You're at the final step! Navigate back to the product creation or editing page on NEXUS." %}

  1. {% trans "Find the '3D Model' field." %}
  2. {% trans "Click 'Choose File' and select the .glb or .gltf file you just exported." %}
  3. {% trans "Save your product. Your new 3D model will now be available on the product detail page!" %}
